About Me

I am a Doctoral Researcher in the Department of Water Resources Development and Management at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee. My research integrates satellite Earth observation, field measurements, machine learning, and deep learning to investigate inland water systems and agricultural sustainability from local to global scales.

My work focuses on inland water mapping and classification, water storage assessment, hydrological applications of satellite observations, and satellite-based agricultural monitoring. I am particularly interested in leveraging artificial intelligence and geospatial analytics to address challenges related to water resources, climate resilience, and sustainable agriculture.

Research

My research combines Earth Observation, hydrological science, and artificial intelligence to better understand inland water dynamics and agricultural systems. My contributions include developing the first global framework for mapping and classifying lentic and lotic inland waters, creating a global inventory of nearly 95 million inland waterbodies, developing the Storage and Reachability Index (StoRI), and contributing to AI-driven agricultural datasets and advisory systems such as IndiaRice500 and CropLizer.

Bio

Ankit Sharma is a Doctoral Researcher in Water Resources Engineering at IIT Roorkee. His work combines satellite remote sensing, machine learning, deep learning, and field observations to study inland water resources and agricultural systems.

His research has resulted in novel frameworks for global inland water mapping, water storage assessment, flood mitigation analysis, and AI-enabled agricultural monitoring. He has authored publications in leading journals and conferences, developed open-source scientific tools, and actively contributes to the international Earth Observation and hydrological science communities.
